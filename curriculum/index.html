Curriculum

We’ve broken down our curriculum into 8 major categories. In each category, the skills are broken down into three groups, corresponding to Level 1, Level 2 and Level 3. Ideally, Level 1 should be completed in its entirety before moving on to Level 2, etc.

Pulse

  • Solid, consistent pulse

  • Varies pulse to fit the music

  • Pulse persists with other body movement

  • Creates pulse in any part of the body

Solo movement

  • Executes some historical solo jazz/blues movements
  • Is aware of center
  • Basic posture

  • Enjoy and feel comfortable dancing alone to blues music

  • Executes basic hip and upper body movement
  • Heightened body awareness - more knowledge and control over body part locations and movement
  • Executes several historical solo jazz/blues movements

  • Understanding lines and energy

  • Integrates complex hip and body movement into partnered and solo dance
  • Thoroughly familiar with all parts of own body & fine control over how all parts move
  • Dances with the whole body (from head to hands to feet)

Rhythm

  • Finds the beat/1 and stays on (or preferably behind) it

  • Able to dance full time (on beat), half time (slows), and double time (quicks) to most music

  • Dances behind the beat

  • Comfortable with multiple rhythms (step hold, quick quick slow, etc), transitions between them, and is able to create own rhythms from on beat, slow, and quick steps

  • Polyrhythms, difficult rhythms
  • Different partners can dance to different rhythms

Connection

  • Basic frame in open, closed, “shut” or “close embrace”
  • Frame not too stiff or too loose (not arm* Y)
  • Leads & follows from the body much of the time

  • Shifts weight clearly & completely

  • Can lead & follow well in open, closed, and shut/close embrace
  • Absorption/elasticity in frame
  • Leads & follows from the body all of the time
  • “Waits” for partner / has delay and goes to the end of movement

  • Able to lead and follow small and large movements

  • Can use any point of connection
  • Can send and receive complex and subtle info through connection
  • Matches partner’s frame
  • Is responsive to partner’s axis and can perform both shared axis and fixed axis (colgadas) turns

Musicality

  • Hits a break

  • Dances to general “feel” of the song

  • Listen to the lyrics

  • Predict where music is going, to be musical in songs they’ve never heard before

  • Can dance well to subtle/simple music
  • Dances to the overall mood of the song, as well as the rises & falls within a song
  • Use their body as an instrument

Vocabulary

  • Basic turns (inside, outside, free)
  • Basic travelling (walking forward, backward, grape* Vining at different tempos)

  • Knows the basic terms/vocab (slow drag, underarm turn, close, open, pulse, etc)

  • Multiple turns, traveling or stationary turns
  • Is able to dance some vocabulary they haven’t been taught (makes some of their own movements)
  • Familiar with historical dances
  • Can travel in a variety of positions/connections/directions

  • Incorporates solo movement into partner dance

  • Has developed personal style/aesthetic
  • Thoroughly familiar with historical dances, and integrates them with their personal style

Texture/Dynamics

  • NA

  • At least 1 variable to texture their dancing corresponding to the music (big vs small, fast vs slow, sharp vs smooth, traveling vs stationary. add others)

  • Employs a wide variety of textures to correspond to the music, or to contrast it

Partnering/Conversation

  • Smiles during the dance
  • Is courteous and respectful

  • Can ask somebody to dance

  • Can execute basic call and response while connected
  • Can break away and come back together comfortably

  • Can share a spin axis comfortably and safely

  • Leads are able to “listen” to follows and know what foot they are on, whether they are on balance, where their hips and upper body are, and hear their “suggestions” (whether or not they are taken)
  • Follows are able to communicate all of the above to the leader, and can make “suggestions” within the lead without taking over
  • Can share weight (counterbalance, dip) comfortably and safely
