The Close Embrace Staircase 2

by Paul Mandel

Emphasize sensitivity, receptivity and consent throughout.

Prerequisites

This works best with students who are already comfortable with the [90-10 rule] and dancing comfortably in close embrace.

Exercise

Start with an introduction to the concept of the close embrace staircase:

  • We can dance close embrace in many different ways. Sometimes, you’re dancing with somebody you know well and you’re both melting into each other and dancing very intimately. Sometimes, you’re dancing with somebody you don’t know as well and your close embrace is much more formal.

  • We can think of close embrace connection as a staircase. The bottom of the staircase is the most intimate/close/melty close embrace you can think of. The top of the staircase is the most formal, impersonal close embrace you can think of.

  • Every single close embrace connection should start at the top of the staircase. As you start dancing together, you can only move at most one step below your partner.

Demonstrate levels 1-10 with your partner, calling out each one.

Have students practice displaying and feeling the levels (but only down to level 6 or so, so avoid uncomfortability).
